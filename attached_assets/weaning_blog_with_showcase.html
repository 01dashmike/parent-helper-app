🥄 Weaning Your Baby: A Science-Backed Guide for First-Time Parents

Weaning—transitioning your baby from milk to solids—is a big step. With a little planning, it can be fun, healthy, and stress-free. Here’s everything you need to know to start confidently, including recipes, free printables, and eco-conscious tips.

📆 When to Start

Most babies are ready around 6 months. Look for signs like sitting up with support, interest in food, and losing the tongue-thrust reflex. Avoid starting before 17 weeks unless advised by a healthcare professional.

🍽️ Weaning Methods

Traditional Spoon-Feeding

This involves offering smooth purees from a spoon and gradually increasing texture as your baby develops chewing skills.

  • Great for introducing iron-rich foods early (e.g. lentils, meat, spinach).
  • Allows you to control ingredients and avoid allergens gradually.
  • Useful for early batch cooking and freezing.

Baby-Led Weaning (BLW)

With BLW, babies feed themselves soft, appropriately sized finger foods from the start, skipping purees altogether.

  • Encourages hand-eye coordination, chewing, and self-regulation.
  • Works well with family meals—just prepare baby-safe portions.
  • Start with steamed carrot sticks, broccoli florets, avocado slices, and soft-cooked pasta.

Mixed Approach

Many families use both methods: spoon-feeding iron-rich purees while offering finger foods for exploration and texture variety.

🧊 Batch Recipes for Weaning

Batch cooking saves time and reduces waste. Try these simple, nutritious options and freeze them in ice cube trays:

  • Sweet Potato Mash: Steam, mash with breastmilk/formula, freeze in cubes.
  • Apple & Pear Purée: Steam until soft, blend together, no added sugar.
  • Lentil & Carrot Purée: Simmer red lentils with chopped carrot and a pinch of cumin.
  • Broccoli & Pea Mash: Steam and blend with a little olive oil for healthy fats.

Pro tip: use silicone ice cube trays and label your freezer bags with chalkboard labels. Use a Magic Bullet Blender for batch prep.

  • Avoid single-use plastic pouches—try reusable food pouches.
